How it works

Five steps from coverage declaration to first activation.

1

Declare your coverage footprint

Tell us which Ottawa FSAs you can serve: K1J, K1K, K1C, K4A, K2J. We match you only to cohorts inside your coverage boundary.

2

Receive matched cohorts

Address-verified, in-coverage, contract-end-aligned demand. No wasted reach — every household in the cohort you see is actionable for your footprint.

3

Place one sealed bid

Submit your $/household/month rate, service terms, and activation timeline. Single round, no counter-bids, no negotiation theatre.

4

We manage the entire cutover

Whollar handles household communications, scheduling, and activation tracking. You receive a clean activation list, not a pile of inbound calls.

5

Pay per activated household

Success fee billed net-30 from each confirmed activation. Nothing on households that don’t activate. Zero cost on a losing bid.

Bid scoring

How bids are scored.

60%

Price to household

The $/month rate you bid for the cohort. The single largest driver of the score. Better price wins more weight than anything else.

25%

Service terms

Contract length, speed tier, and SLA guarantees. Longer contracts with stronger SLAs score higher on this dimension.

Activation speed

Your committed timeline from cohort award to household activation. Faster commitments win. Clawback applies if you miss the committed window.

Risk terms

Risk terms, plainly stated.

1

Service SLA

Your SLA commitment starts day 30 of activation. Any SLA gaps inside that window are recorded and affect future cohort eligibility.

2

Success fee timing

Fee invoiced net-30 from each individual activation date. You are never charged in advance or in bulk before activations are confirmed.

3

90-day churn clawback

If a household churns within 90 days of activation, the success fee for that household is refunded in full. No disputes — automatic.

4

Activation rate expectation

Historical activation rates are 80–90% across comparable cohort pilots. The fee is charged only on confirmed activations regardless of the final rate.

5

Cohort exclusivity

One provider wins each cohort. No household is sold to two providers. The winning bid is exclusive for the full activation window.

Fair by design

The rules of the game are transparent.

🔒

Sealed bids

No one sees competing bids before the auction closes. One round, no counter-offers, no advantage to being last.

🏠

Cohort exclusivity

The winning provider owns the cohort activation window. No household is pitched by a second provider after the auction closes.

🔐

Consent-first data

All household data collected under opt-in consent, fully PIPEDA and Québec Law 25 compliant. Shared only with the winning provider.

🎯

You set your floor

Your minimum acceptable bid rate is set in your provider profile. You are never pushed below your floor by the scoring algorithm.

FAQ

Common questions from providers.

Nothing to apply and nothing to place a bid. The success fee of $40–80 per activated household is the only charge, and it applies only when a household activates. Losing bids cost zero. There is no subscription, no listing fee, and no payment before activation is confirmed.

Each household completes an address qualification step that confirms they are at a serviceable Ottawa address. They provide explicit opt-in consent under PIPEDA, along with their current plan details and contract end date. Only verified, consented households are included in a cohort. The data you receive as a winning provider reflects this full verification process.

Whollar manages all household communications, scheduling, and coordination from auction close through to activation confirmation. You handle the technical activation — provisioning, installation, or self-serve setup depending on your product — and we track each household’s status and report activations to you in real time.

Winning providers receive: full name, service address, FSA, current provider, contract end date, and current monthly bill for each household in the cohort. This data is shared exclusively with the winning provider after the auction closes and is governed by the terms of your founding-partner agreement.
