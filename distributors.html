How bids are scored

A transparent auction. Published rubric.

60%

Price to household

The monthly $/mo you're offering households. The largest factor — but not the only one. Set a rate you can sustain, not just the lowest number you can write down.

25%

Service terms

Speed tier, contract length, data caps. Households in Ottawa cohorts skew toward families who care about reliability. Fibre + no data cap beats a cheap but throttled plan.

15%

Activation speed

How quickly you can activate after winning. If you can begin within 14 days rather than 30, that matters to households who've been waiting — and it shows in your score.

All bidders see this rubric before submitting. No black box. You know exactly how your bid will be scored before you place it.

Your SLA

Transparent commitments, both directions.

1

SLA begins day 30

You have 30 days after winning to begin activation. Whollar gives you the full cohort list and contact details on day one. Falling behind triggers a review.

2

Fee on activation only

$40–80 per household, confirmed in your partner agreement. Zero if the household doesn't activate. No upfront spend, no cost on losing bids.

3

90-day churn clawback

If a household cancels within 90 days of activation, the success fee for that household is reversed on your next invoice. You pay for retention, not churn.

4

Net-30 payment

Whollar invoices monthly based on verified activations. No prepayment. No retainer. Clear records you can reconcile against your own billing system.

5

80–90% activation expected

Based on household pre-commitment. You won't be surprised — every household opted in with their current bill and contract window on file before the auction opens.
